Greenhouse environment

Our plants live in a health spa, 24 hours a day.

Step inside a B.C. glass greenhouse and you'll find a perfect tropical oasis — managed by science and technology, growing produce that's picked perfect.

A perfect oasis

Where science turns into art.

When you step inside a greenhouse, you'll feel like you've stepped into a perfect tropical oasis managed by science and technology. We like to say that our plants live in a health spa 24 hours a day.

Our growers have perfected the growing environment for our tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers and lettuce. Yes, our crops look and taste perfect — but it's not because we've done anything unnatural to them.

Our growers don't use waxes. The B.C. greenhouse vegetables you eat are just as they were when we picked them off the plants: perfect. If you spent 24 hours a day in a health spa, you'd look perfect, too.

Did you know?

Greenhouses take up just 0.01% of B.C.'s farmland — but produce 11% of its total agriculture production.

The perfect region

Nature's ideal growing conditions.

Our skilled growers have turned science into art. They give our plants exactly what they need — the perfect climate, the right amount of light, the right amount of nutrition, exact amounts of water and carbon dioxide, and the proper ventilation.

The cooling ocean breezes and many hours of sunlight make the southern coast of British Columbia the perfect region in the world for greenhouse vegetable production. Take a step into one of our glass greenhouses and you'll find it's an oasis.

Why greenhouses?

Growing more, more responsibly.

A greenhouse lets our farmers control the climate 24 hours a day, so plants grow strong, healthy and big — while we make the most of B.C.'s land.

Grown hydroponically

Farmers grow plants in rockwool and biodegradable material such as sawdust and coconut fibre. Plants stretch their roots and are fed water and nutrients directly where they need it — only as much as they need for optimum growth.

No herbicides

A plastic sheet suppresses weeds from growing up through our crops. At the end of each season it's removed for a thorough cleaning — ensuring no pests or diseases carry into the new growing season.

World leaders in pest management

To keep pests at bay we use biological controls. Our growers introduce predatory insects — like ladybugs — and mites to eliminate harmful pests, meaning our greenhouses use little to no pesticides.

B.C.'s future food supply

We can grow 10 to 20 times the amount of vegetables on the same area of field — feeding millions of people healthy, fresh vegetables virtually year-round.
